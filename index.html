Currency Denomination Reader

Built a currency denomination reader for the visually impaired as a class project in ENG 10 at De Anza College, in partnership with Infineon Technologies. The project involved testing Infineon components and providing user feedback throughout development. The device uses a push-button connected to a Raspberry Pi and camera housed in a 3D-printed enclosure. When a user places a bill inside and presses the button, the camera captures an image, which is processed using OpenCV and compared against a library of known currency denominations. The system supports U.S., Canadian, and British bills. The goal of this project was to provide an accessible and low-cost solution for users—particularly in developing regions—who may not have access to smartphones or advanced assistive technologies, helping them identify currency safely and independently.
